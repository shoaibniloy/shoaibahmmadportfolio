F1 Debris Detection on Track using YOLOv11n

Overview:

This project developed a real-time object detection system using YOLOv11n (You Only Look Once version 11n) to enhance Formula 1 race safety by detecting critical objects on tracks, such as debris, marshals, and recovery vehicles. Leveraging advanced computer vision, the system identifies potential hazards like tire fragments or broken car parts during live races, enabling swift responses to ensure safety.

Key Highlights:

  • YOLOv11n Model: Utilized for its speed and accuracy, ideal for real-time object detection in dynamic environments.
  • Training Dataset: Trained on Roboflow's annotated dataset, including images of debris, marshals, and recovery vehicles for effective feature learning.
  • Real-World Testing: Successfully tested on 1080p F1 POV video footage, detecting high-speed debris and stationary hazards with robustness.

Performance Metrics:

  • Precision & Recall: Achieved 96% precision for recovery vehicles and high precision for marshals, with overall recall at 83%. Debris recall improved at higher confidence levels.
  • Confusion Matrix: High accuracy for recovery vehicles and marshals, with minimal misclassification for background and debris.
  • Precision-Recall Curve: Near-perfect precision for recovery vehicles and steady improvement for debris and marshals.
  • Training Metrics: Significant reduction in box and classification losses, with continuous improvement in mean average precision (mAP).
